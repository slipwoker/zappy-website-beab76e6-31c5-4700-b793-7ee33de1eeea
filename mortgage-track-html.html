Fixed Rate Index-Linked

A mortgage track where the interest rate remains fixed throughout the loan period, while the principal is linked to the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

  • Stable monthly payments adjusted for inflation
  • Protection against interest rate fluctuations
  • Ideal for long-term financial planning
  • Popular choice among Israeli homebuyers
üí∞

Prime Rate Track

A variable interest rate mortgage linked to the Bank of Israel's prime rate, offering flexibility and potential savings when rates decrease.

  • Interest rate changes with market conditions
  • Lower initial rates compared to fixed tracks
  • Suitable for short to medium-term loans
  • Requires monitoring of rate changes
üîí

Fixed Rate Non-Linked

A mortgage with a fixed interest rate that is not linked to any index, providing complete payment certainty throughout the loan period.

  • Absolute payment stability
  • No exposure to inflation or currency changes
  • Predictable monthly payments
  • Higher initial interest rates

Foreign Currency Track

A mortgage denominated in foreign currency (usually USD or EUR), offering potential benefits but with currency exchange risk.

  • Lower interest rates than shekel tracks
  • Exposure to currency exchange fluctuations
  • Suitable for those with foreign income
  • Requires careful risk assessment

Choosing the Right Track

Selecting the appropriate mortgage track depends on various factors including your financial situation, risk tolerance, income stability, and long-term plans. Our expert consultants analyze your unique circumstances to recommend the optimal combination of mortgage tracks that will minimize your costs and provide the security you need.

Mix and Match Strategy

Most borrowers benefit from combining multiple mortgage tracks to balance risk and opportunity. A typical strategy might include 40% fixed index-linked for stability, 30% prime rate for flexibility, and 30% fixed non-linked for certainty. We help you create a customized portfolio that aligns with your financial goals and market conditions.
